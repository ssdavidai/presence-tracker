# Metrics Reference

All five per-window metrics are bounded **0.0 → 1.0**. Higher is not universally better or worse — each metric has a directional interpretation listed below.

---

## CLS — Cognitive Load Score

**Range:** 0 (idle) → 1 (maximum load)

**What it measures:** How mentally demanding was this 15-minute window?

**Inputs:**
- Calendar: `in_meeting`, `meeting_attendees`, `meetings_count`
- Slack: `total_messages`, `messages_sent`
- RescueTime: `app_switches`, `productivity_score`
- Calendar: back-to-back meeting penalty

**Interpretation:**
- 0.0–0.3 — light load, idle or low-demand work
- 0.3–0.6 — moderate, normal productive work
- 0.6–0.8 — high, meetings or intense context switching
- 0.8–1.0 — peak load, likely unsustainable

---

## FDI — Focus Depth Index

**Range:** 0 (fragmented) → 1 (deep flow)

**What it measures:** How deep and uninterrupted was your focus?

**Inputs:**
- RescueTime: `focus_seconds`, `distraction_seconds`, `app_switches`
- Slack: penalised by high send volume (interruption indicator)
- Calendar: penalised for meetings (context breaks)

**Interpretation:**
- 0.8–1.0 — deep flow, minimal interruption
- 0.5–0.8 — moderate focus, some context switching
- 0.0–0.5 — fragmented, hard to sustain work

---

## SDI — Social Drain Index

**Range:** 0 (isolated) → 1 (maxed out socially)

**What it measures:** How much social energy was spent?

**Inputs:**
- Calendar: `meeting_attendees`, `meetings_count`
- Slack: `messages_sent` (active participation)
- Omi: `word_count`, `duration_seconds` (ambient conversation)

**Interpretation:**
- High SDI + low recovery → social fatigue warning
- SDI is not intrinsically bad; it's a signal to correlate with RAS

---

## CSC — Context Switch Cost

**Range:** 0 (no switching) → 1 (maximum fragmentation)

**What it measures:** Fragmentation penalty — how much context switching occurred?

**Inputs:**
- RescueTime: `app_switches` (primary driver)
- Slack: active in multiple channels simultaneously
- Calendar: back-to-back meetings with different topics

**Interpretation:**
- High CSC consistently → indicates flow-killing work patterns
- Correlates inversely with FDI in healthy work days

---

## RAS — Recovery Alignment Score

**Range:** 0 (misaligned) → 1 (perfectly aligned)

**What it measures:** Is your workload appropriate for your physiology today?

**Inputs:**
- WHOOP: `recovery_score` (primary)
- CLS for the window (computed first, then fed into RAS)

**Formula:** Smooth `tanh` curve comparing CLS against WHOOP recovery tier.

| Recovery tier | Expected behaviour |
|--------------|-------------------|
| **HIGH** (≥75%) | High CLS is fine — push today |
| **MODERATE** (50–74%) | Moderate CLS acceptable; heavy load penalised |
| **LOW** (<50%) | Any significant load reduces RAS sharply |

**Interpretation:**
- RAS close to 1.0 = you're working at the right intensity for your body
- RAS close to 0.0 = you're pushing hard on a recovery day, or coasting on a green day

---

## DPS — Daily Presence Score

**Range:** 0–100

**What it measures:** Single composite number summarising cognitive quality of the day.

**Formula:** Weighted blend of the five metrics across all active windows:

| Metric | Weight |
|--------|--------|
| FDI | 30% |
| RAS | 25% |
| CLS (inverted) | 20% |
| CSC (inverted) | 15% |
| SDI (inverted) | 10% |

**Interpretation:**
- 80–100 — excellent cognitive day
- 60–79 — solid, normal productive day
- 40–59 — moderate; some overload or fragmentation
- 0–39 — heavy load day or recovery mismatch

---

## CDI — Cognitive Debt Index

**Range:** 0–100 (higher = less debt)

**What it measures:** Multi-day rolling fatigue balance.

**Formula:** Rolling average of RAS × (1 - normalised CLS) over 7 days, smoothed and scaled to 0–100.

**Interpretation:**
- 85–100 — recovery consistently ahead of load; no debt
- 60–84 — small deficit forming; watch for sustained high CLS
- 0–59 — cognitive debt accumulating; reduce load or prioritise recovery sleep

CDI is reported in the morning brief and nightly digest alongside the day's DPS.
